## I. INTRODUCTION

Defendant Northwind Dynamics, LLC ("Northwind") respectfully moves this Court to
dismiss the Complaint in its entirety pursuant to Rule 12(b)(6) for failure to
state a claim upon which relief can be granted. Plaintiff's trade-secret and
breach-of-contract theories rest on conclusory allegations that, even accepted as
true, do not establish the elements of either claim.

## II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

The parties executed a mutual NDA on 2024-09-12. Plaintiff alleges that a former
employee, hired by Northwind in 2025, disclosed proprietary actuator designs.
The Complaint, however, identifies no specific trade secret, no reasonable
measures taken to protect it, and no factual basis connecting any Northwind
product to Plaintiff's designs.

## III. ARGUMENT

**A. The Complaint fails to identify a protectable trade secret with particularity.**
A plaintiff must describe the alleged trade secret with sufficient specificity to
separate it from matters of general knowledge. Plaintiff's references to
"advanced actuator technology" are precisely the kind of sweeping, undifferentiated
assertions courts routinely reject.

**B. Plaintiff pleads no reasonable measures to maintain secrecy.**
Absent well-pleaded facts of access controls, confidentiality markings, or
need-to-know restrictions, the information cannot qualify for protection.

**C. The breach-of-contract claim is duplicative and inadequately pled.**
The contract claim merely restates the trade-secret theory and fails to allege a
specific breached provision or resulting damages.

## IV. CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Northwind respectfully requests that the Court grant
this Motion and dismiss the Complaint with prejudice.
